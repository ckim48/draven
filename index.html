About Me

Building Systems That Bend, Not Break

Draven is a Taiwanese-American designer and researcher focused on creating adaptive systems that strengthen vulnerable communities. Drawing from his family's legacy of community service and his deep connections to Taiwan's indigenous populations, he works at the intersection of architecture, policy, and social innovation. His projects range from modular disaster relief shelters inspired by traditional craftsmanship to economic policy initiatives supporting single mothers. Through research published in international conferences and hands-on community work, Draven develops solutions that honor cultural heritage while addressing modern challenges. His approach combines technical innovation with social responsibility, always asking how design can serve those who need it most.

Research

Ongoing projects and published work exploring culture, architecture, and linguistics.

Optimizing Thermal & Environmental Performance

  • Field measurements using thermocouples, hygrometers, and anemometers.
  • Comparative analysis of traditional design efficiency.

Spatial Perceptions of Traditional Taiwanese Design

Comparative study between Taiwan and Taiwanese-American contexts.

  • Image evaluations: harmony, openness, utility, and comfort.
  • ANOVA analysis of traditional elements like wooden screens and gardens.

Tone & Stress Patterns in Taiwanese Aboriginal Languages

Cross-linguistic phonological study.

  • Audio elicitation tasks and pitch contour comparisons.
  • Perception tests with native and non-native speakers.

Language Revitalization & Cultural Identity

  • Surveys on proficiency and cultural engagement.
  • Ethnographic interviews capturing youth narratives.
  • Statistical evaluation of revitalization program outcomes.
